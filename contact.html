Export & Shipping FAQ

Important details concerning container loads, custom packaging, payment safety, and product certification.

What are the Minimum Order Quantities (MOQs)? +

Because we focus on commercial sea freight shipping, our standard minimum order quantity for clay bricks and flooring tiles is **one 20ft Full Container Load (FCL)**, which holds approximately 19 to 21 tons depending on port restrictions. For wood pellets, we accept orders starting from **20 metric tons** (shipped in bulk containers or jumbo bags).

What payment structures do you support for exports? +

To ensure transaction security for both parties, we transact using **Irrevocable Letters of Credit (L/C at sight)** issued by reputable Tier-1 international banks, or **Telegraphic Transfers (T/T)** with a 30% deposit upfront and the remaining 70% payable immediately upon presentation of the scanned Bill of Lading (B/L) and export invoice papers.

Which shipping Incoterms do you export under? +

We primarily quote prices on a **FOB (Free On Board)** basis at our main Vietnam or UK loading ports. However, we are fully equipped to arrange ocean transit and insurance, providing **CIF (Cost, Insurance & Freight)** or **CFR (Cost and Freight)** quotes to your designated port of discharge.

Do you supply material samples and technical test sheets? +

Yes. We provide sample boxes of tiles and bricks for architectural inspection and mechanical testing. The product samples are supplied free of charge, though we request that the courier freight costs (via DHL, UPS, or FedEx) be paid by the recipient. Chemical assays and thermal reports for wood pellets are sent digitally on demand.
